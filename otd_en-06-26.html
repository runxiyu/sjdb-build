June 26

  • 1409 – The Council of Pisa elected Peter of Candia as Pope Alexander V, becoming the third simultaneous claimant of the title of leader of the Roman Catholic Church.
  • 1844 – Julia Gardiner Tyler married John Tyler at the Church of the Ascension in New York City and became the First Lady of the United States.
  • 1889 – Bangui, the capital and largest city of the present-day Central African Republic, was founded in French Congo.
  • 1997 – J. K. Rowling's Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone, the first book in the Harry Potter fantasy novel series, was released.

Births and Deaths: Marie Thérèse Geoffrin (b. 1699); Mary van Kleeck (b. 1883); Grand Duchess Maria Nikolaevna of Russia (b. 1899); Olive Morris (b. 1952)
